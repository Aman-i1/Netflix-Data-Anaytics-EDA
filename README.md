# 🎬 **Netflix Data Analysis (Exploratory Data Analysis)**

---

## 📌 **Project Overview**

This project presents a detailed **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on a real-world **Netflix Movies & TV Shows dataset**.

The main objective is to analyze content trends and uncover key insights about:

- 📊 **Content distribution patterns**
- 🎥 **User-oriented content strategy**
- 🌍 **Global production trends**

The analysis explores **content type, genre, duration, ratings, and geographic distribution** to identify meaningful patterns driving Netflix’s content ecosystem.

## 🎯 **Key Research Questions**

This analysis answers the following important questions:

- **Content Type:** Are Movies more dominant than TV Shows on Netflix?  
- **Genre Popularity:** Which genres are most common on the platform?  
- **Release Trends:** How has content production evolved over the years?  
- **Geographical Contribution:** Which countries contribute the most content?  
- **Duration Analysis:** What is the typical duration of movies and TV shows?  
- **Ratings Insight:** What type of audience (kids, teens, adults) is primarily targeted?  
- **Seasonality:** Are there specific months when more content is added?  
- **Correlation Analysis:** Is there any relationship between release year, duration, and ratings?

## 📂 **Dataset Description**

The dataset contains structured information about Netflix titles:

- **Total Records:** 8,807  
- **Total Columns:** 12  

### 🔑 Features:

- **Title**
- **Type (Movie / TV Show)**
- **Director**
- **Cast**
- **Country**
- **Release Year**
- **Date Added**
- **Rating**
- **Duration**
- **Genre (Listed In)**
- **Description**

---

## 📊 **Data Overview**

- Majority of data is **categorical**
- Only **release_year** is numerical
- Dataset size: ~825 KB  

### ⚠️ Missing Values:

- **Director:** ~29.9% missing  
- **Cast & Country:** ~9% missing  
- Other columns are mostly complete  

---

## 📈 **Key Insights**

### 🎥 Content Distribution
- Movies dominate with **6,131 titles**
- TV Shows account for **2,676 titles**

---

### 🎭 Genre Trends
- Most popular genre: **International Movies**
- Followed by:
  - Dramas
  - Comedies
- Multi-genre classification is widely used

---

### 📅 Release Trends
- Very low content before 2000  
- Rapid growth after 2015  
- Peak around **2018–2019**

---

### 🌍 Geographical Insights
- **United States** leads content production  
- **India** is the second-largest contributor  
- Followed by **United Kingdom**

---

### ⏱️ Duration Analysis
- Average movie duration: **~100 minutes**
- Most movies fall between **80–120 minutes**
- Most TV Shows have **1 season**

---

### ⭐ Ratings Distribution
- **TV-MA (Mature Audience)** dominates (~38%)
- Followed by:
  - TV-14 (~25%)
- Platform mainly targets **adult & teen audience**

---

### 📆 Content Addition Trends
- Massive growth after **2016**
- Peak additions in **2019–2020**
- **July & December** have highest additions

---

### 🔗 Correlation Analysis
- Weak relationships between variables:
  - Release Year vs Duration → Slight negative correlation  
  - Release Year vs Rating → Very weak positive  
- Overall: **Features are mostly independent**

---

### 🎬 Audience Engagement Insights
- Most common words in descriptions:
  - *Life, Love, Family, Young*
- Indicates focus on:
  - Relationships
  - Personal journeys
  - Real-life themes

---

## 🧠 **Business Insights**

- Netflix focuses heavily on **international and diverse content**
- Strong shift toward **mature audience (TV-MA)** after 2015  
- Preference for:
  - Shorter movies  
  - Limited series (1 season)  
- Multi-genre tagging improves **content discoverability**

---

## 🚀 **Conclusion**

This EDA reveals that Netflix has evolved into a **global, data-driven streaming platform** that:

- Prioritizes **international expansion**
- Focuses on **adult-oriented content**
- Uses **multi-genre classification** for better recommendations
- Adapts content duration for **modern viewing habits**
